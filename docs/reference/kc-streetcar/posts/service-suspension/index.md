# Back in Service

Source: https://kcstreetcar.org/service-suspension/

### KC Streetcar service resumed September 13 ahead of schedule

The KC Streetcar is back in service! After 5 days of preventative track maintenance activities and Riverfront Extension overhead wire work, streetcars started rolling through downtown first thing Saturday morning.

Since September 8, crews worked 12-hour shifts, including nights, to complete this critical work ahead of schedule. Favorable weather conditions also played a key role, especially for concrete pouring.

Throughout this week crews completed:

- Installation of expansions joints on Delaware Street between Independence Avenue and 7th Street.
- Reconstruction of track slab on Delaware Street.
- Installation of new roadway markings to improve motorist and streetcar safety along Delaware Street between Independence Avenue and 7th Street.
- Mill and overlay of the intersection of 3rd Street and Grand Boulevard.
- A wide variety of overhead wire installation related to the Riverfront Extension project on Grand Boulevard between 2nd and 3rd Street, and near the KC Streetcar maintenance facility.
Between September 8 – 14, a dedicated “Streetcar Link” bus service was deployed , maintaining public transportation coverage along the streetcar route throughout the temporary suspension. This bus service operated during regular streetcar hours with frequencies of approximately 15-20 minutes.
