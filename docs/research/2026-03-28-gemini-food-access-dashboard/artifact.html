Kansas City Food Access Solution Research

The Challenge: Kansas City faces rising food insecurity, grocery store closures, pantry strain, and transportation barriers. The goal is to match supply, logistics, access, and adoption in a scalable way.

Identify U.S. city-scale programs that address food access. Prioritize operational, measurable solutions realistic for a 60-day pilot.

⚠️ Key Constraints

Must be deployable in 60 days. Cannot rely on slow infrastructure builds (e.g., new grocery stores). Must show proof of impact quickly.

🔍 Focus Areas

Distribution coordination, pantry discovery, resident alerts, hub scheduling, and volunteer logistics.

Kansas City 60-Day Pilot Concepts

Three actionable, realistic concepts derived from the "High Transferability" models above. Each can be executed within a 60-day timeline.

Primary Recommendation

SMS Pantry Reservation + Discovery Engine

Why it's the winner for KC:

  • ✓ Speed: Can be deployed using white-labeled SaaS (like Plentiful) or simple Twilio logic in under 30 days.
  • ✓ Solves Strain: Eliminates physical lines at pantries, smoothing the operational curve for overwhelmed staff.
  • ✓ Low Barrier: SMS requires no smartphone data plan, app download, or high tech literacy.
  • ✓ Measurable: 60-day impact is easily proven by "number of reservations processed" and "reduction in wait times."

Execution Plan (60 Days):

  1. Days 1-10: Secure buy-in from 3-5 high-volume KC pantries.
  2. Days 11-25: Configure white-label SMS reservation platform. Map pantry schedules.
  3. Days 26-40: Train pantry staff/volunteers on the intake tablet/dashboard.
  4. Days 41-60: Launch localized marketing (flyers at pantries, transit stops) & run the pilot. Track diverted line-hours.
