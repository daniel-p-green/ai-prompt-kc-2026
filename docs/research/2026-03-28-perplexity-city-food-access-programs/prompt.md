# Source Prompt

You are a policy + product researcher helping build a Kansas City food access solution for the National Prompt Championship challenge.

Context:
- Kansas City faces rising food insecurity, recent grocery store closures, pantry strain, and transportation barriers.
- The working concept may involve food distribution coordination, pantry discovery, resident alerts, hub scheduling, volunteer logistics, and proof of impact within 60 days.
- The challenge is not just “more food.” It is matching supply, logistics, access, and adoption in a way a city could fund and renew.

Research goal:
Identify how other U.S. cities or city-scale programs have addressed food access in ways relevant to Kansas City. Prioritize solutions that are operational, measurable, and realistic for a 60-day pilot.

Questions to answer:
1. What intervention models have cities actually used?
   - centralized referral / coordination platforms
   - mobile markets
   - pantry finder / resident navigation tools
   - transit-adjacent or transit-enabled food distribution
   - last-mile delivery partnerships
   - volunteer logistics / food rescue networks
   - healthy corner store programs
   - public-private grocery or market models
2. For each example, what exact problem did it solve?
3. What operating model made it work?
   - city-led, nonprofit-led, public-private, health system partnership, etc.
4. What evidence of traction exists?
   - households served, orders, pounds distributed, repeat use, cost, grants, expansion
5. What constraints or failure points showed up?
   - staffing burden, subsidy dependence, SNAP friction, data quality, cold chain, adoption
6. Which patterns are most transferable to Kansas City in 60 days?
7. Which ideas are attractive but likely too slow, too expensive, or too dependent on policy change?

Output format:
- Start with a table of 8-12 comparable programs.
- Columns: City, Model, Lead organizations, User served, Key mechanic, Evidence/results, Risks, Transferability to KC (High/Medium/Low), Why.
- Then summarize:
  - Top 5 reusable patterns for Kansas City
  - Top 5 anti-patterns / traps
  - 3 pilot concepts Kansas City could plausibly execute in 60 days
  - 1 recommended concept with rationale
- Use primary sources when possible: city departments, nonprofits, universities, journals.
- Include links for every example.
- Distinguish clearly between proven results, operator claims, and your inference.
- Do not romanticize “food desert” solutions that rely on building a full new grocery store unless there is strong evidence they can launch fast.
