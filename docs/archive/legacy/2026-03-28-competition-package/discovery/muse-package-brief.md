# Muse Package Brief

Date: 2026-03-28
Repository: ai-prompt-kc-2026
Concept: StreetCart KC
Source of truth: `docs/product/oracle-muse-fact-base.md`

## Summary

Muse should make StreetCart KC feel like a serious Kansas City civic program with visible momentum, not like a startup mascot or a sports gimmick. The creative layer must inherit the Oracle facts directly:

- the five locked pilot ZIPs
- the four locked hub zones
- the partner-supported operating model
- the “streetcar is the public spine” message
- the top-line household outcome metrics

The right creative posture is:

- civic utility first
- local pride second
- sports energy third

## Non-Negotiables

- Keep households served and priority ZIPs visible.
- Do not show food moving on board the streetcar.
- Do not make the wrap concept look like a billboard sale.
- Do not mimic the live arrival tracker so closely that it feels fake or technical.
- Use one or two hard facts per artifact, not a dashboard.

## Brand Direction Brief

### Positioning

StreetCart KC is the visible access layer that makes Kansas City’s food-response system feel coordinated, local, and real.

### Tone

- Practical
- Urgent
- Proud
- Neighbor-first
- Confident without being cute

### Visual direction

- Use KC Streetcar blue as the anchor color.
- Add produce green and warm gold as support colors.
- Use route lines, stop markers, and simple scoreboard modules as recurring shapes.
- Photography and mock scenes should show hubs, volunteers, produce, and pickup activity, not generic smiling crowds.

### Suggested palette

| Token | Color | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Streetcar blue | `#0081C6` | headline accents, route line, primary CTA |
| Charcoal | `#1E2430` | type and dark scoreboard surfaces |
| Warm cream | `#F4F0E6` | background and print collateral base |
| Produce green | `#2E7D32` | success, produce, household outcome accents |
| Alert red | `#C62828` | urgency, supply shortfall, curveball alerting |
| Signal gold | `#F2C14E` | scoreboard highlights and achievement states |

### Verbal system

- Primary line: `One line. More access.`
- Secondary line: `StreetCart KC uses the streetcar corridor as the public spine while partners do neighborhood fulfillment.`
- Proof line: `600 household pickups or deliveries in 60 days.`

## Scoreboard Mock Spec

### Purpose

Show that the bracket and public display exist to reinforce household outcomes, not to distract from them.

### Format

- `16:9` landscape
- styled like a transit information board, not a sports broadcast lower-third
- dark base with bright route-line and KPI accents

### Required modules

1. Header: `StreetCart KC Live Scoreboard`
2. Topline KPI: `Households Served`
3. Secondary KPI: `Priority ZIP Share`
4. Current service status: next active hub windows
5. Team standings: only top three teams
6. Footer rule: `Food access first. Competition second.`

### Required content

- `600 household pickups or deliveries in 60 days` as the campaign proof target
- the five pilot ZIPs in a compact line
- a clear note that points are earned only from verified service

### What to avoid

- fake vehicle telemetry
- dense leaderboards
- more than two hard numbers above the fold

## Corridor + ZIP Callout Visual Spec

### Purpose

Help judges understand how a visible north-south line can support service into ZIPs that do not all sit directly on the tracks.

### Composition

- Put the streetcar line in the center as one bold north-south spine.
- Mark the four operating hubs:
  - River Market Exchange Hub
  - Union Station Civic Hub
  - Guadalupe Family Hub
  - Cross-Lines Connector Hub
- Place the five pilot ZIP callouts outside the line with short feeder arrows.
- Add one short caption: `Streetcar spine, partner fulfillment, ZIP-targeted access.`

### Hard facts to include

- `1 in 7` Kansas Citians are food insecure
- `3 million pounds` lost from federal cuts

Do not add more data than that to this visual.

## Wrap Concept Brief

### Purpose

Develop a renewal-phase wrap concept that celebrates public impact, not sponsorship inventory.

### Source constraints

Use the KC Streetcar wrap guidance and eligibility material in:

- `docs/reference/kc-streetcar/downloads/Streetcar-Wrap-Guidelines-2025.pdf`
- `docs/reference/kc-streetcar/downloads/KC-Streetcar-Wrap-Categories-Design-Guidelines-2023_Finals.pdf`

### Concept direction

Theme: `Kansas City Moves Together`

### Message hierarchy

1. StreetCart KC name
2. Household outcome message
3. Priority ZIP visibility
4. Partner acknowledgement
5. Renewal-phase celebration language

### Visual cues

- full-length route line running through the body of the vehicle
- large ZIP badges or neighborhood markers
- produce and pantry imagery kept graphic and simple
- no corporate logo dominance

### Rule

Call the wrap a renewal-phase reward or celebration asset in all Oracle and Muse language.

## Resident Flyer + SMS Copy Pack

### Flyer headline

`StreetCart KC`

### Flyer subhead

`One line. More access.`

### Flyer body copy

StreetCart KC helps Kansas City families get clear food pickup or delivery instructions through a visible citywide program. The streetcar corridor is the public spine. Pantry and nonprofit partners help move food into priority ZIP codes. Pickup comes first. Delivery is available when transportation barriers make pickup fail.

### Flyer proof block

- 5 priority ZIPs
- 4 recurring hub zones
- bilingual pickup guidance
- verified household outcomes
