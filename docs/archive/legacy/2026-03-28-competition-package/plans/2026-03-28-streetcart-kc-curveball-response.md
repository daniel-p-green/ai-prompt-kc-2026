## Why This Strengthens The Pitch

StreetCart KC should treat the curveball as proof that the same architecture handles both community trust and produce triage. The streetcar remains the public spine. Partner hubs remain the fulfillment layer. Volunteers remain the repeatable labor pool. The only thing that changes is which operating lever moves first.

## Locked Facts

- Community-vote ZIPs: `66101` and `66105`
- Vote window: `14 days`
- Produce surge: `1,000 lbs` in a `48-hour` perishables window
- KCK bracket identity: `KCK Wildcats`
- Internal reallocation for vote support: `$8K`
- Emergency produce move cost: `~$190`

## Community Vote Response

### Objective

Build resident support in `66101` and `66105` without changing the pilot model. The vote is a trust campaign first, not a software problem.

### Trusted anchors

- El Centro Academy parent pickup line in `66101`
- Cross-Lines Community Outreach in `66105`
- Guadalupe Centers pantry hours for bilingual outreach support
- After the Harvest KC route touchpoints in both ZIPs

### 14-day sequence

1. Days 1-3: embed with trusted anchors
   - station bilingual volunteers at El Centro Academy pickup
   - pair Cross-Lines distribution with support forms
   - use Guadalupe Centers pantry hours for “taste and vote” outreach
2. Days 4-10: run `Voto y Verdura` pop-ups
   - place produce-bag tables at school, transit-adjacent, church, and laundromat or tienda-adjacent sites
   - attach one bilingual support form and one phone-first information card to each bag
3. Days 11-13: canvass the densest blocks
   - use two-person bilingual teams
   - focus on the densest block groups in each ZIP
   - use one script in Spanish and English, not multiple variants
4. Day 14: submit and publicly thank
   - deliver the support tally
   - hold a small thank-you moment at the anchor sites

### Design rules

- Spanish-first headline and body; English second
- Bilingual hotline or text line, not QR-first outreach
- Canvassers should come from community networks rather than downtown volunteers
- `KCK Wildcats` is the bracket identity used in KCK-facing volunteer copy

## Produce Surge Response

### Objective

Move produce before spoilage by routing first to the strongest cold-chain partners, then to same-day or next-day distribution.

### Allocation plan

| Destination | Pounds | Why it gets volume first | Cold chain |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Cross-Lines Community Outreach | 400 | Supports the active KCK vote ZIPs and has the strongest KCK perishables fit | Walk-in cooler |
| Guadalupe Centers | 200 | Spanish-first community fit and fast turnover | Fridge, short hold |
| Della Lamb Community Services | 150 | Additional short-hold refrigerator capacity and refugee-services reach | Fridge |
| Harvesters Community Food Network | 250 | Industrial reserve and next-route release capacity | Industrial cold storage |

### 48-hour sequence

1. Day 0
   - pick up, sort, bag, and route to cold-chain partners
   - use the first volunteer wave as a preseason scrimmage
2. Day 1
   - run any same-day pop-up release where partner staffing allows
   - keep overflow at Cross-Lines or Harvesters
3. Day 2
   - clear remaining stock through scheduled partner distribution and route releases

### Volunteer framing

- message: “Emergency food run”
- goal: move perishables, test dispatch, and create proof that StreetCart KC works under time pressure
- staffing shape: drivers, sorters, and partner-site handoff crews
