# PART 1: THE CONCEPT

## StreetCart KC in One Sentence

StreetCart KC converts Kansas City's fare-free streetcar corridor into a gamified food access backbone, using sports-team volunteer tournaments to push grocery boxes from transit hubs into the city's worst food deserts for $20-35 per delivered box.

## The Three-Layer Architecture

**Layer 1: The Spine (Streetcar Corridor)**
The KC Streetcar runs 5.7 miles fare-free from River Market to UMKC with ~30 stops and 10,000+ weekday riders. The corridor has 25+ interactive digital kiosks with 55-inch screens designed for civic content. The streetcar authority already supports non-transit programming (Art in the Loop, Holiday Jam, sports team vehicle wraps, sponsored community campaigns). StreetCart KC uses this corridor as a public scoreboard and logistics staging trunk. It does NOT use the streetcar as a freight vehicle.

**Layer 2: The Tournament (Engagement Engine)**
KC sports teams already run deep food-insecurity partnerships (Chiefs Kingdom Food Drive, Royals Respond Fund, Sporting KC milk donations, KC Current streetcar wraps). StreetCart KC organizes volunteer delivery into team-based brackets. Fans sign up under their team, claim delivery routes, and earn points for completed deliveries. Standings update in near-real-time on streetcar kiosks. The winning team earns a custom streetcar wrap for a full season. This solves the #1 failure mode in volunteer food logistics: churn.

**Layer 3: The Last Mile (Volunteer Delivery into Priority ZIPs)**
Streetcar stops serve as staging hubs. Volunteers pick up sorted grocery boxes at 3-4 high-footfall stops (River Market, Union Station, Westport, Plaza) and deliver them into priority ZIPs east of the line (64127, 64128, 64130, 64132) and across the river in KCK (66101, 66105). Partner pantries (Bishop Sullivan, Guadalupe Centers, Crosslines, Della Lamb, El Centro) serve as neighborhood anchors. The worst food deserts sit east of the streetcar, not on it. The streetcar is the scoreboard and staging trunk. The volunteers are the last mile. That separation is by design.

## Why It Works (Evidence Base)

The strategic blueprint for Kansas City food access rates three intervention patterns as "High Transferability":
1. Algorithmic Volunteer Matching (micro-volunteering via app) -- StreetCart KC's core delivery mechanic
2. Digital Scheduling for Physical Distribution -- StreetCart KC's kiosk and dispatch system
3. Transit-Adjacent Pop-Up Co-location -- StreetCart KC's hub-and-spoke staging model

The blueprint's own recommended concept ("KC Route-Rescue") is structurally identical to StreetCart KC minus the streetcar spine, the scoreboard, and the engagement engine. StreetCart KC adds the civic and motivational layer that the blueprint identifies as necessary but cannot provide.

The blueprint also identifies five anti-patterns that StreetCart KC explicitly avoids:
1. Subsidizing brick-and-mortar grocery ($29M Sun Fresh collapse) -- StreetCart KC has zero real estate
2. High-CAPEX mobile markets ($102K+ per bus) -- StreetCart KC uses volunteer personal vehicles
3. Assuming high digital literacy (Baltimore's 85% non-redemption) -- recipients get a doorbell and a box, not an app
4. Ignoring cold-chain logistics (47% damaged food in gig delivery) -- StreetCart KC budgets $15K for insulated totes/coolers
5. Reliance on perpetual corporate subsidies -- StreetCart KC models true per-delivery cost from day one

## Curveball 1: Community Vote in 66101 + 66105

### The Facts
- City council requires resident support (not just orgs) in Zones 2-3
- ZIPs 66101 (62.8% Hispanic, 24,500 pop) and 66105 (71.4% Hispanic, 18,200 pop)
- Vote deadline: 14 days from March 28

### The Response: "Voto y Verdura" (Vote and Vegetables)

**Days 1-3:** Station bilingual volunteers at El Centro Academy school pickup (2:30-3:30 PM) and Crosslines distribution hours. Explain program, collect support signatures at the point where trust already exists.

**Days 4-10:** Run 6-8 pop-up produce giveaways at El Centro parking lot, Minnesota Ave bus stop (t-007), Crosslines (66105), laundromats, and tiendas. Every bag includes a Spanish-first card explaining StreetCart KC + a simple support form.

**Days 11-13:** Door-to-door bilingual canvass teams (2-person pairs) in densest block groups. 200 doors per ZIP over 3 days. Canvassers recruited from El Centro parent networks, not from downtown.

**Day 14:** Submit resident tally. Celebrate at anchor sites.

**Critical design decisions:**
- All materials Spanish-first, English-second (not side-by-side bilingual)
- Phone number for questions, not website (51% no-vehicle rate correlates with limited smartphone access)
- Add "KCK" bracket team for Wyandotte volunteers (do not force KCK residents into Missouri sports affiliations)
- Dedicated bilingual community liaison on staff ($4K/60 days, funded from staff line)

**Budget impact:** $8,000 absorbed from existing community outreach and contingency lines. 1.6% of total spend to secure political authorization for the other 98.4%.

# PART 6: THE ARCHITECT (Dashboard Spec)

## What to Build
A single interactive web dashboard that serves as both the public-facing kiosk scoreboard and the funder-facing data tool.

## Components

1. **Tournament Scoreboard** - Team standings (deliveries, pounds, households) with team colors. This is what renders on kiosks.
2. **ZIP Code Heat Map** - Color-coded map showing delivery density across priority ZIPs, overlaid with food desert data from the challenge food-atlas API.
3. **Live Feed** - Rolling feed of completed deliveries with team attribution.
4. **Supply Alert Banner** - Pulls from supply-alerts API. Currently shows TEFAP cuts, 311 demand spike, community vote requirement, and emergency produce donation.
5. **Budget Tracker** - Spend vs. budget by category, cost-per-delivery trend.
6. **KPI Cards** - Households served, pounds moved, volunteer count, on-time rate, equity percentage (% to priority ZIPs).
7. **Pantry Network Map** - All 12 pantries from the API with cold storage indicators, language capabilities, and capacity.

## Tech Stack
- React + Tailwind CSS
- Pulls from all 8 challenge API endpoints
