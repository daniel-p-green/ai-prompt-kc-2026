# Executive Deck Outline

Date: 2026-03-28
Repository: ai-prompt-kc-2026
Concept: StreetCart KC
Audience: judges, city stakeholders, and operators

## Slide 1: The Decision

**Title:** Kansas City Has a Food Access Problem. StreetCart KC Fixes The Access Layer.

**Core message:**  
Kansas City does not need a new grocery store in 60 days. It needs a faster way to coordinate food, people, and places.

**Proof points:**
- Two grocery stores closed in the last year
- The main food bank lost 3 million pounds from federal cuts
- 1 in 7 Kansas Citians are food insecure[^1]

**Visual:**  
One strong headline, one city map, one short thesis

## Slide 2: What Is Actually Broken

**Title:** This Is A Coordination Problem, Not Just A Supply Problem

**Core message:**  
Food exists in the system, but access breaks across transport, language, scheduling, and fragmented referrals.

**Proof points:**
- Pantry schedules can change
- Current resident tools are fragmented across multiple services and referral paths[^2][^3]
- Store-first solutions move too slowly for a 60-day proof window[^4][^5]

**Visual:**  
Simple before-state flow with too many disconnected steps

## Slide 3: The Insight From Other Cities

**Title:** Cities Win With Coordination, Trusted Sites, And Narrow Delivery

**Core message:**  
The strongest city models use neighborhood pickup points, transit-adjacent access, centralized coordination, and selective delivery.

**Benchmarks to name:**
- Baltimore Virtual Supermarket[^6]
- Fresh MARTA Market, Atlanta[^7]
- FAST, Philadelphia[^8]
- CDC produce-box model[^9]

**Visual:**  
Four-tile benchmark grid with one sentence per city

## Slide 4: The Solution

**Title:** StreetCart KC

**Core message:**  
StreetCart KC is a thin coordination-and-fulfillment layer that helps partners schedule recurring hubs, guide residents to the next best option, and move perishables fast.

**Three parts:**
- recurring neighborhood hubs
- bilingual resident navigation
- targeted last-mile delivery

**Visual:**  
Simple system diagram, not UI yet

## Slide 5: Why The Streetcar Still Matters

**Title:** The Streetcar Is The Public Spine. Partners Do The Fulfillment.

**Core message:**  
The streetcar gives the city a visible, ownable, Kansas City-specific program spine. Bus access and partner sites extend reach beyond the corridor.

**What to say out loud:**
- We are not relying on streetcars to carry food
- We are using the corridor to organize attention, hubs, and trust
- We use bus access and partners to reach farther

**Visual:**  
Streetcar corridor with feeder arrows into target ZIPs

## Slide 6: The 60-Day Pilot

**Title:** Start Narrow, Prove Fast

**Core message:**  
Launch three to four recurring hubs in priority ZIPs and measure completed access, not app traffic.

**Pilot scope:**
- ZIPs: 64130, 64127, 66101, 66105, 64132
- weekly recurring hub windows
- pickup first, selective delivery second
- bilingual SMS and partner-assisted intake

**Visual:**  
Pilot map and 60-day timeline

## Slide 7: How The Curveball Makes Us Better

**Title:** The Model Already Handles Language And Perishables

**Core message:**  
The curveball is not a detour. It is a test of whether the model actually works.

**Show:**
- Spanish-language outreach in the 80% Spanish-speaking zone
- 14-day vote support through partner channels and SMS
- 48-hour produce allocation through produce boxes or produce bags[^9]

**Visual:**  
Three-column response plan

## Slide 8: Unit Economics And City ROI

**Title:** Use The $500K To Prove Renewal, Not To Build Fixed Assets

**Core message:**  
The contract supports a thin product, partner operations, outreach, and proof. It should not fund heavy infrastructure.

**What to show:**
- $500K revenue / $150K direct operating-cost frame[^1]
- spend concentrated in coordination, outreach, and execution
- renewal logic tied to households served, produce moved, and partner participation

**Visual:**  
Simple budget split and ROI logic

## Slide 9: What Judges Can Touch

**Title:** Show The Operating System In Under Two Minutes

**Core message:**  
The demo should prove the system, not impress with features.

**Demo path:**
1. Operator schedules a hub
2. Resident receives bilingual instructions
3. Produce gets allocated or delivery gets assigned
4. Public scorecard updates

**Visual:**  
Three-screen storyboard

## Slide 10: The Ask

**Title:** Fund The Access Layer

**Core message:**  
StreetCart KC gives Kansas City a faster way to coordinate food access with visible proof in 60 days.

**Close with three reasons:**
- it uses existing infrastructure and partners
- it solves the actual access problem
- it gives the city a credible renewal case

**Last line:**  
Do not fund another fragmented program. Fund the operating layer that makes the existing system work better.
